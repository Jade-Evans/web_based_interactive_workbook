Practicing Creating SVGs

SVGs stand for Scalable Vector Graphics.

They are a type of image format, commonly used for: icons, graphs/charts, simplified images.

They are scalable imaged defined by Math, as opposed to rasterised images defined by grids of pixels.

This means they do not blur or lose quality when enlarged, however they are not the correct image format for detailed or photorealistic imagery, as defining this would be excessively complicated.

SVGs are defined using .XML (Extensive MarkUp Language) which is interpolable with HTML. This means defining an svg image in HTML will enable that image to be rendered.

.XML is also human-readable.
